Prof. Zhihong Chen & Prof. Peter Bermel
Purdue University · Birck Nanotechnology Center · ECE Department

Abstract

This research prototypes and refines specialized 2.5D Heterogeneous Integration (HI) processes for embedding millimeter-level commercial chiplets — specifically d-latch memory microchips and photodiodes — onto a silicon-based interposer for low-earth orbit (LEO) satellite system applications. By leveraging silicon's unique material properties and the structural advantages of 2.5D packaging, the system achieves reduced interconnect lengths, lower power consumption, and improved signal integrity. Through extensive cleanroom prototyping and iterative process optimization, this work aims to deliver a reliable and cost-effective packaging solution for next-generation space electronics.

Overview & Motivation

Modern satellite systems demand compact, power-efficient electronics capable of operating reliably in the harsh conditions of low-earth orbit — including intense radiation, vacuum, and extreme thermal cycling. Traditional single-chip solutions struggle to meet the combined requirements of high integration density, low power, and design flexibility.


2.5D Heterogeneous Integration addresses these challenges by enabling distinct chiplets — each optimized independently — to be co-packaged on a common silicon interposer. This research investigates reliable methods for physically securing commercial off-the-shelf (COTS) dies within etched silicon cavities, establishing stable electrical interconnections suitable for space-grade deployment.

Fabrication Process Flow

1

Substrate Patterning

Photolithographic pattern-etching of the silicon carrier substrate to create precise die cavities.

2

Copper Seed Layer

Deposit a thin copper seed layer across substrate gaps to enable subsequent electroplating.

3

Electroplating

Grow copper through electroplating to mechanically secure chiplets within the cavities.

4

Insulation Layer

Deposit dielectric insulation to protect the foundation layer from shorting.

5

Interconnect Patterning

Deposit photoresist and copper, pattern redistribution connections, metallize signal traces.

6

Final Lift-Off

Lift-off process to define and expose electrical connection pins for external interfacing.
